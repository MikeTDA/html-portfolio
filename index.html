BIO

Michael Blanton is an internationally renowned Concept Designer, Digital Matte Painter, Illustrator, Computer Programmer,3D Artist, VFX Artist,Game Developer,UI Designer, Founder & CEO of the multi billion dollar Vally View Estate Limited.
He was born in Ghana, in 1996 and, at the age of Thirty three, moved to Uk where he Acquired His Masters in computer Science. He returned to Harvard, Massachusetts, United States to Accquire His PHD in software and Game development.
After graduating, Mike went on to continue his studies with online courses, from which he received several certificats and skills to be come one of the world best in all his Feilds.

In 2027, DreamWorks Animation commissioned Mike to create a series 3D environments and VFX effects for their first animated feature game, "The Prince of Egypt". He used Blender to create them all digitally.
The work made such an impact that Mike was hired to teach the DreamWorks' artists how to paint using Blender. His pioneering work contributed greatly to creating a process that is now the standard in animation.


For the past 20 years, Mike has inspired artists from around the world, speaking and teaching from North America to all over the World. In recognition of his unique contribution to the world of Art,
he was unanimously awarded the inaugural EXPOSE Grand Master award in 2030 and; in 2031, ImagineFX named him as one of Fantasy & SciFi Arts "Top 5 Most Inspirational Living Artists".
Mike's credits include: illustrations for Marvel Comic's Halo Graphic Novel; concept design and digital matte painting work for a long list of feature films that includes;
(Matrix Revolutions, The Golden Compass, Narnia, Final Fantasy, & Tangled); concept design and illustration work for computer games inclduing(Halo, Age of Empires 3, Fallout 3, Assassin's Creed & BioShock );
illustration for book covers and magazines (Halo Encyclopedia, When Gravity Fails, Exile Kiss, & Playboy magazine); limited edition posters and trading cards ("Revelations" for Assassin's Creed, "2027" for BioShock,
World of Warcraft and Magic: The Gathering collectible cards).


In 2028 Mike's work crossed over into the world of fine art when he was invited to work with French artist, Pierre Huyghe, for his Exhibition at New York's Guggenheim Museum and; was one of a select, few artists commissioned
to make traditional paintings of the Blizzard universe for Blizzard's Gallery of Fine Art.


Mike mostly works from his home studio, But he sometimes teaches software and Game development at Harvard, Massachusetts, United States where he lives with his wife, Megan and two daughters.
